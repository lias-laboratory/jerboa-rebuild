\section{Introduction}

%%% Introduce the topic ==> geometric modeling softwares
Nowadays, geometric modelers are everywhere to be found, for example, in
archaeology, architecture and mechanical engineering, just to name a few
domains. Their importance in design and conception has grown to the point to
where they are %make them
essential. These softwares rely on geometric modeling kernels to maintain the
designs and allow precise and complex works.

%% Topological changes tracking is our main interest
Topological changes (or \emph{events}) tracking is a key feature %functionality
of these kernels such as (\textit{ACIS}, …). Such a feature %functionality
is necessary %shows its importance
during the construction of a model when the applied operations modify both % its
geometry and topology. Considering how models tend to grow in size during their
conception, the tracking may slow down over time and the more elaborated the
model, the more coherence issues may arise.
%% We know models grow and become large. Dynamically tracking events may not be ideal
In its dynamic conception, since it requires to compare a model before and after the
application of an operation, event tracking may become a heavy task to perform and
its time cost may become significant.
%% We propose a method to statically detect events to alleviate the cost of tracking them
Our intent here is to propose to alleviate this time cost by analyzing
operations rather than the model before and after their application. Operating
in such a way also allows to detect events before actually using an operation
and would be beneficial in preventing inconsistency.
