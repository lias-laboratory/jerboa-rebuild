	%% Abstract
	\begin{abstract}
		\begin{linenumbers}
			Designing a 3D parametric model is a tedious process of trial and error. %
			To make the designing process easier, current parametric modeling systems
			offer a rebuild feature that allows users to edit the design process of a
			model and regenerate it. %
			However, most modeling systems require tracking numerous topological
			entities and only consider editing from the standpoint of geometric
			parameters, thus limiting rebuilding capabilities. %

			In this paper, we present a rebuild mechanism that tracks only necessary
			topological entities and allows users to add and delete operations as part
			of the editing process. %
			To this end, our approach relies on two formalisms to represent objects with
			generalized maps and define modeling operations with graph transformation
			rules. %
			It enables both a homogeneous and persistent naming of topological entities. %
			Moreover, operations can also be syntactically analyzed to detect
			topological changes, or \emph{events}, such as the creation, deletion or
			merging. %
			Thus, our rebuild mechanism can automatically track topological entities
			during both the build and rebuild processes. %
			Altogether, it enables reconstituting a topological entity's history,
			annotating relationships between entities and events across operations, and
			matching equivalent candidate entities when rebuilding. %

			Finally, our rebuild mechanism allows users to explicitly choose match
			candidates and keep control on the rebuild process.
		\end{linenumbers}
	\end{abstract}
